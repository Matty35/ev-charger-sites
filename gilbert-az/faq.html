Rebates & Incentives

Yes. Arizona Public Service (APS) currently offers a $250 rebate for residential customers who install a qualifying Level 2 (240V) EV charger. Eligibility requirements include:

  • Must be an active APS residential customer at the installation address
  • The charger must be a Level 2 (240V) EVSE unit
  • Installation must be performed by a licensed electrical contractor
  • The charger must be UL-listed and meet APS specifications

We file the entire APS rebate application on your behalf, at no extra cost. Most customers receive their rebate check within 4–6 weeks of the approved application.

The Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (IRS Form 8911), established under the Inflation Reduction Act, allows homeowners to claim 30% of the cost of EV charging equipment and its installation. The maximum credit is $1,000 per year for residential filers. This credit applies at your primary or secondary residence. We provide itemized receipts and documentation to support your tax claim. Consult a tax professional to confirm your specific eligibility.

Installation Process & Permits

Yes. The Town of Gilbert requires an electrical permit for EV charger installations that involve new circuits, wiring modifications, or breaker panel changes. This is standard practice across Arizona municipalities. Installing a charger without a permit is against local code and can create serious problems — including insurance issues and complications when selling your home. Gilbert EV Charge handles all permitting on your behalf, including application, inspection scheduling, and final sign-off. This service is included in every installation at no extra cost.

Most residential Level 2 charger installations take 2 to 4 hours from start to finish. Charger Types & Brands

There are three main levels of EV charging:

  • Level 1 (120V): Uses a standard household outlet. Delivers 3–5 miles of range per hour. Good for plug-in hybrids or emergency charging, but too slow for most full EV owners.
  • Level 2 (240V): Requires a dedicated 240V circuit, the same voltage as a dryer or range. Delivers 15–35 miles of range per hour. This is the gold standard for home and workplace charging.
  • DC Fast Charging (Level 3): Commercial-grade chargers found at public charging stations. Delivers 100–350 miles of range per hour. Not practical for residential installation due to very high cost and power requirements.
