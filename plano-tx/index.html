Frequently Asked Questions — EV Charger Installation Plano, TX

EV charger installation in Plano typically costs between $799 and $1,800 for a residential Level 2 charger. The final cost depends on your electrical panel capacity, the distance from the panel to the installation location, conduit requirements, and the charger unit you choose. After the 30% Federal Tax Credit (up to $1,000), many homeowners significantly reduce their out-of-pocket cost. You should also ask your Retail Electric Provider (TXU Energy, Reliant, etc.) about any EV rate plans available to you. Commercial installations are priced separately based on the number of units and infrastructure scope.

Yes. Under the Inflation Reduction Act, homeowners can claim a 30% Federal Tax Credit (up to $1,000) on the cost of EV charging equipment and installation at their primary or secondary residence. Plano EV Charge provides itemized receipts and all required documentation to help you claim this credit at no extra charge. We recommend consulting a qualified tax professional to confirm your eligibility.

Most residential Level 2 charger installations are completed in 2 to 4 hours. This includes installing the dedicated 240V circuit breaker, running conduit and wiring, mounting the EVSE unit, and testing the system. If your electrical panel needs an upgrade or the wiring run is unusually long, it may take slightly longer. Commercial multi-unit installations typically take one to two days.

Yes. The City of Plano Building Inspections Division requires an electrical permit for EV charger installations involving new circuits or wiring modifications. Installing a charger without a permit can create safety hazards, void your homeowner's insurance, and cause problems when you sell your home. Plano EV Charge handles all permit applications, required inspections, and final sign-off as part of every installation project — at no extra cost to you.

Absolutely. We install Tesla Wall Connector Gen 3 units regularly throughout Plano and North Texas. The Gen 3 Wall Connector delivers up to 48 amps (11.5 kW) and can add up to 44 miles of range per hour for compatible Tesla vehicles. We handle the 240V circuit, wall mounting, and connector configuration. The Tesla Wall Connector is compatible with all Tesla models and, with the included adapter, supports J1772 vehicles as well.

We install all major EVSE brands, including ChargePoint Home Flex, JuiceBox 40/48, Emporia Smart EV Charger, Grizzl-E Classic and Smart, Enel X JuiceBox, and Tesla Wall Connector. We also install customer-supplied chargers, as long as they are UL-listed and meet NEC requirements. We can help you choose the right unit for your vehicle, budget, and usage patterns.

Plano is served by Oncor Electric Delivery for the electric wires and distribution infrastructure. However, because Texas has a deregulated electricity market, you choose your own Retail Electric Provider (REP) such as TXU Energy, Reliant, Green Mountain Energy, or others. Some REPs offer special EV rate plans with lower overnight rates — ask your REP about available EV plans when you have your charger installed.
