Why Frisco Homeowners Are Installing Level 2 Chargers

Frisco is one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States, and its residents are adopting electric vehicles at a pace that far outstrips the DFW average. With major employers like Toyota North America and Liberty Mutual drawing tech-forward professionals to the area, and with neighborhoods like Starwood, Stonebriar, and Frisco Lakes filled with large homes and 3-car garages, the conditions for home EV charger installation have never been better.

A standard 120V household outlet (Level 1) delivers only about 4–5 miles of range per hour of charging. For most Frisco commuters — many of whom drive 30–60 miles round-trip to Dallas, Plano, or Allen — that's simply not fast enough. A Level 2 charger running on 240V delivers 25–37 miles of range per hour, meaning most EVs go from near-empty to full overnight.

The math is simple: a Level 2 charger transforms your EV from a vehicle you have to carefully manage to one that's always ready the moment you need it.

The City of Frisco Building Inspections division requires an electrical permit for Level 2 EV charger installation. Here's how we handle it:

  • Most Frisco permits are approved within 1–3 business days
  • We coordinate the final inspection appointment around your schedule
  • After the inspector signs off, we provide you with your permit completion certificate

Federal Tax Credit & Oncor Rebates for Frisco Residents

Frisco homeowners have access to two layers of financial incentives that significantly reduce the net cost of EV charger installation:

Federal 30% Tax Credit: Under the Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (IRS Form 8911), you can claim 30% of the total cost of equipment and installation, up to $1,000 for residential installations. Since Texas has no state income tax, this federal credit is especially valuable for Frisco residents.

Oncor Utility Rebates: Oncor Electric Delivery, which provides electrical service to Frisco, has offered rebates for qualifying EV charging equipment. We stay current on Oncor's active programs and submit your application as part of every installation at no extra charge.

When both incentives are combined, many Frisco homeowners see their effective out-of-pocket cost drop by 40–60% compared to the sticker installation price.
