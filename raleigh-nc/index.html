Frequently Asked Questions — EV Charger Installation Raleigh, NC

EV charger installation in Raleigh typically costs between $799 and $1,800 for a residential Level 2 charger. The final cost depends on your electrical panel capacity, the distance from the panel to the installation location, conduit requirements, and the charger unit you choose. After the $200 Duke Energy Progress rebate and 30% Federal Tax Credit, many homeowners pay as little as $350–$900 out of pocket. Commercial installations are priced separately based on the number of units and infrastructure scope.

Yes. Duke Energy Progress offers a $200 rebate for qualifying EV charger installations at residential properties in their service territory, which includes Raleigh, NC. To qualify, the charger must be a Level 2 (240V) unit, and installation must be completed by a licensed electrical contractor. Raleigh EV Charge handles the entire rebate application process for our customers at no additional charge — we submit all required documentation on your behalf.

Most residential Level 2 charger installations are completed in 2 to 4 hours. This includes installing the dedicated 240V circuit breaker, running conduit and wiring, mounting the EVSE unit, and testing the system. If your electrical panel needs an upgrade or the wiring run is unusually long, it may take slightly longer. Commercial multi-unit installations typically take one to two days.

Yes. The City of Raleigh requires an electrical permit for EV charger installations involving new circuits or wiring modifications. Attempting an installation without a permit can create safety hazards, void your homeowner's insurance, and cause problems when you sell your home. Raleigh EV Charge handles all permit applications through City of Raleigh Inspections & Permits, required inspections, and final sign-off as part of every installation project — at no extra cost to you.

Absolutely. We install Tesla Wall Connector Gen 3 units regularly throughout Raleigh and the Triangle area. The Gen 3 Wall Connector delivers up to 48 amps (11.5 kW) and can add up to 44 miles of range per hour for compatible Tesla vehicles. We handle the 240V circuit, wall mounting, and connector configuration. The Tesla Wall Connector is compatible with all Tesla models and, with the included adapter, supports J1772 vehicles as well.

We install all major EVSE brands, including ChargePoint Home Flex, JuiceBox 40/48, Emporia Smart EV Charger, Grizzl-E Classic and Smart, Enel X JuiceBox, and Tesla Wall Connector. We also install customer-supplied chargers, as long as they are UL-listed and meet NEC requirements. We can help you choose the right unit for your vehicle, budget, and usage patterns.

Under the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), homeowners can claim a 30% Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit on the cost of EV charging equipment and its installation, up to a maximum of $1,000 per year for residential installations. This credit applies to installations at your primary or secondary residence in the United States. We provide itemized receipts and documentation to make claiming this credit straightforward. We recommend consulting a qualified tax professional to confirm your eligibility.
