EV Charger FAQ — Overland Park & Johnson County, KS

Real answers to the questions Overland Park homeowners ask most about EV charger installation, Evergy rebates, permits, and costs.

Evergy Rebates & Federal Incentives

Evergy offers a rebate to residential customers in its Kansas service territory — which includes Overland Park and all of Johnson County — who purchase and install a qualified Level 2 EV charger at their primary residence. To qualify, you must be an active Evergy customer, install an ENERGY STAR certified Level 2 charger, and submit the required documentation including proof of purchase, installation invoice, and device model information within the program deadline.

Overland Park EV Charge manages this entire process for you at no additional cost. We ensure the charger we install is on Evergy's qualified product list and submit all paperwork on your behalf. Learn more about Evergy rebates.

The Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (IRS Form 8911) allows homeowners to claim 30% of the cost of qualified EV charger equipment and installation labor, up to $1,000 for residential installations. This is a tax credit — not a deduction — meaning it directly reduces your federal tax liability dollar for dollar.

To claim the credit, your charger must be installed at your primary or secondary residence and placed in service during the tax year you are claiming. We provide a detailed, itemized invoice showing equipment cost and labor cost separately, which is the documentation your tax preparer or CPA will need to file Form 8911. Consult your tax advisor to confirm your specific eligibility.

Yes. The Evergy rebate and the Federal 30% Tax Credit can be combined, making home EV charger installation in Overland Park significantly more affordable than most homeowners expect. For example, on a $1,500 total installation (equipment + labor), you might receive $250 from Evergy's rebate and claim up to $375 in federal tax credits, bringing your effective out-of-pocket cost below $900. We walk every customer through this calculation during the quoting process.

Installation & Permits

Yes. The City of Overland Park requires an electrical permit for any 240V Level 2 EV charger installation. After installation, a city electrical inspector must sign off on the work. This protects homeowners by ensuring the work meets the National Electrical Code and local amendments. Overland Park EV Charge handles all permit applications and schedules the required inspection — you do not need to do anything.

Most residential installations in Overland Park are completed in 2 to 4 hours. This assumes a standard attached garage with accessible panel location and adequate existing capacity. If conduit must be run through walls or outdoors, or if a panel upgrade is required, the job may take 6 to 8 hours or extend to two visits. We give you a realistic timeline estimate during your free site assessment so there are no surprises.

Yes, in most cases. A 100-amp panel can often accommodate a 30A or 40A Level 2 charger circuit if there is adequate spare capacity. For a 48A circuit (the maximum for most home Level 2 chargers), a 200-amp panel is typically required. Options include a full panel upgrade (usually $1,500–$3,000 depending on scope), installation of an intelligent load management device that shares power between the charger and high-draw appliances, or a smart charger with built-in power sharing. We assess your specific panel during the free site visit and recommend the most cost-effective solution for your situation.

Kansas does not currently have a statewide "right to charge" law that overrides HOA restrictions, unlike some other states. However, many HOAs in Johnson County communities are permissive of EV charger installations in private garages, provided the installation follows city electrical codes and does not modify shared property. We recommend reviewing your HOA's CC&Rs and, if needed, submitting an architectural modification request before scheduling installation. Our team can provide documentation about the installation scope to support your HOA application.

Charger Types & Brands

Level 1 chargers plug into a standard 120V household outlet and deliver approximately 1.2 to 1.8 kW of power — adding only 4 to 7 miles of range per hour. Most EVs have a 60+ kWh battery, meaning a full Level 1 charge from empty can take 40 to 80 hours. Level 2 chargers use a 240V circuit (the same as a dryer or range) and deliver 7.2 to 11.5 kW — adding 20 to 35+ miles of range per hour. A full Level 2 charge typically takes 6 to 12 hours, making it practical to charge overnight and wake up with a full battery every morning.

We are brand-agnostic and recommend based on your EV, usage habits, and budget. For most Overland Park homeowners, we commonly install ChargePoint Home Flex (48A, excellent app), JuiceBox 48 (smart scheduling, Evergy-compatible), Emporia EV Charger (budget-friendly, solid app), Wallbox Pulsar Plus (compact, smart), and Tesla Wall Connector for Tesla owners. All of these qualify for the Evergy rebate and the Federal Tax Credit. We stock all major brands and can usually install within 3 to 5 business days of your quote.

Public DC fast chargers and Level 2 stations in the Overland Park area — near major destinations and employers like the former Sprint and T-Mobile campuses — are convenient for emergencies or road trips but are poor substitutes for daily home charging. Public chargers are frequently occupied, sometimes out of service, and charge at a significantly higher per-kWh rate than your Evergy residential rate. With a home Level 2 charger, you plug in every night and wake up to a full battery every morning — no detours, no waiting, no uncertainty. The convenience and cost savings pay back the installation cost within 2 to 3 years for most Overland Park EV drivers.

Cost & Financing

The total installed cost — including equipment and labor — ranges from $800 to $2,500 for most Overland Park residential installations. The main cost drivers are: the charger model selected ($300–$650), the distance from the panel to the install location, whether conduit must be run through walls or outdoors, and whether a panel upgrade is required. After the Evergy rebate and the Federal 30% Tax Credit, many homeowners' effective out-of-pocket cost is in the $600–$1,400 range. Use our cost calculator or call us for a precise quote.
