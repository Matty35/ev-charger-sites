Why Gilbert Homeowners Switch to Level 2 Home Charging

If you own an electric vehicle in Gilbert, Arizona, and you're still relying on a standard 120V household outlet to charge it, you're leaving significant convenience on the table. A Level 1 charger delivers just 3 to 5 miles of range per hour — meaning a 75 kWh battery like the one in a Tesla Model Y or Ford Mustang Mach-E would take 30 to 40 hours to charge from empty. That's not practical for everyday life.

A Level 2 home charger, by contrast, operates on a dedicated 240V circuit — the same voltage used by your electric dryer or range — and delivers 15 to 35 miles of range per hour depending on the charger's amperage and your vehicle's onboard charger capacity. For most Gilbert drivers, that means waking up to a fully charged EV every single morning, no matter how far you drove the day before.

Whether you're driving to work near the Intel or TSMC campuses north of Gilbert, running errands along E. Ray Road, or making the commute into Phoenix and back, a Level 2 home charger transforms your EV ownership experience. You never visit a public charging station for routine top-ups again.

The APS Rebate and Federal Tax Credit

Gilbert homeowners served by Arizona Public Service (APS) may be eligible for a $250 rebate on a qualifying Level 2 EV charger installation. Additionally, the Inflation Reduction Act provides a 30% Federal Tax Credit (up to $1,000) on the combined cost of equipment and installation. Combined, these incentives can reduce your total investment by $400 to $1,200 or more.

We handle the APS rebate application from start to finish — no forms for you to fill out, no documentation to track down. We've completed this process for dozens of Gilbert homeowners and know exactly what APS requires for quick approval.
